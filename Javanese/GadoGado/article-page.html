Gado Gado

Gado-Gado is a traditional Indonesian dish made with lightly cooked or fresh vegetables, tofu, boiled egg, and a rich, creamy peanut sauce. It's healthy, filling, and easy to make on a tight budget, with ingredients that are flexible and widely available.

Information

Total Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 5 Servings

Directions

  1. Step 1

    Prepare the Vegetables: Boil or steam cabbage, carrots, green beans, and bean sprouts until slightly tender (2â€“4 minutes). Drain and let cool. Boil potatoes until soft, then slice. Slice cucumber (if using) and arrange fresh.

  2. Step 2

    Cook the Tofu and Eggs: Fry or boil tofu until golden, then cube it. Boil eggs, peel, and cut in half.

  3. Step 3

    Make the Peanut Sauce: SautÃ© minced garlic and chili in a pan. Add peanut butter, sugar, soy sauce, and tamarind paste or lime juice. Gradually stir in water and simmer until smooth and thick. Adjust salt or sweetness to taste.

  4. Step 4

    Assemble the Gado-Gado: Arrange all vegetables, potatoes, tofu, and egg on a plate. Pour peanut sauce over the top. Sprinkle with fried shallots if available.
